    Sunrisers Hyderabad v Kolkata Knight Riders

    It took Sunrisers Hyderabad 10 games last year to find the right formula. Even though that's quite late in an IPL season, they still had time to turn their campaign around and make a late dash into the playoffs for the fifth consecutive time. The usual suspects delivered, but more importantly, some new heroes emerged which convinced the management to not make wholesale changes to the squad ahead of the auction in February this year. The likes of Jason Holder, Sandeep Sharma, Wriddhiman Saha stepped up to aid skipper David Warner and trumpcard Rashid Khan last year, whereas T Natarajan's meteoric rise grabbed headlines. Add a fit-again Bhuvneshwar Kumar to the mix, SRH are primed to hold on to their reputation of having the best bowling attack. With the stars aligning towards the back end of the tournament last year, 2021 promises more than just a top 4 finish to cap off this successful cycle. Kolkata Knight Riders, on the other hand, had endured a frustrating season six months ago in the UAE but their recent additions give them hope. Shakib Al Hasan, who was a part of the side that won in 2012 and 2014, is back. They have also added Harbhajan Singh to their ranks and will rely on the veteran on slow tracks. Varun Chakravarthy was a revelation last year but they will hope for more wickets from Pat Cummins up front. Eoin Morgan's first full season as KKR skipper is bound to cause excitement and he will certainly pin his hopes on star allrounder Andre Russell, who had a forgettable campaign with the bat last time around.

    Date:11/APR/2021
    Time:7:30 PM GMT
    Venue:MA Chidambaram Stadium, Chennai
